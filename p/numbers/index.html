Numbers
From Fakepedia, the made up encyclopedia

Numbers are cryptic texts used by Mathematicians to obfuscate their communications. They have been used since 874[what is this?] AD and haven't been decoded since.

Characteristics
The true meaning of numbers is unknown.
They use arabic numerals, suggesting that they might have originated in Egypt.
A handful of other symbols have been noticed as well, such as +, -, :, /, ·, % and =.
Sometimes these numerals may appear above other numerals: 23; and on each side of a slash: 2/3
Greek symbols, such as π, φ, Δ, Σ, θ and ψ, can be occasionally seen, which points to a connection to the mathematicians of Ancient Greece, that existed prior to the invention of arabic numerals.

Related terms
Often when discussing numbers, mathematicians mention algebra, geometry, statistics, linear algebra and calculus.
Complex numbers are often utilized, which seems to be an additional layer of encryption by utilizing 2 numerals instead of one. Quaternions are less used, but are likely another increase in encryption, as they use 4 numerals at once. They are likely used for protecting especially sensitive data.
